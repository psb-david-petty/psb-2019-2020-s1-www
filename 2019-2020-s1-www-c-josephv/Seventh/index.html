THE LEGEND OF ZELDA: BREATH OF THE WILD

The greatest video game ever created.

ACCOLADES

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild is a 2017 Nintendo game for the Nintendo Switch and Wii U, It is widely apprasied, loved by critics and fans alike. Breath has received over 200 game awards to date, including multiple awards at The Game Awards 2017. These include Best Game Direction, Best Action/Adventure Game, and Game of the Year. It was also nominated for Best Art Direction, Best Score/Music, and Best Audio Design. Due to it's critical sucess and the massive fanbase for The Legend of Zelda franchise, Breath of the Wild has sold nearly 15 million copies, making it the Switch's 4th best selling game.

S T O R Y

In the game you play as Link, the young hero of the land known as Hyrule, a once flourishing kingdom sprawling with life and peace. 10,000 years before the game takes place, an incarnation of Link defeated the evil and powerful foe Ganon, and with the help of the princess, an incarnation of Zelda, seals him away. Fast forward to 100 years before the game, Ganon has returned, powerful as ever in the form of Calamity Ganon. Link is defeated in combat, and must heal at the Shrine of Ressurection for 100 years. He wakes up to a Hyrule left in shambles from the destruction of Calamity Ganon. He must act quickly, and defeat the antagonist at Hyrule Castle. In the center of the kingdom, Princess Zelda awaits her savior at the castle, needing the aid of the courageous hero.

G A M E P L A Y

Breath of the Wild is such an innovative game because it truly broke the standards of Zelda, and video gaming in general. The devs said that their goal when design BOTW was to return to the style of gamelpay that they created in the original 1986's "The Legend of Zelda" on the NES. In this game, the player was free to choose their path and adventure any way they liked. The game was what is known as "open world" a style of video games that allows the player to approach the game in any way they like. Ever since then, Zelda games have folowed a linear gameplay format, in which different sections of the game are unlocked slowly over time. After oveer 30 years of Zelda, Nintendo finally released another open world game, Breath of the Wild. The gameplay is so fluid and natural, with subtle control reminders that can be toggled off for more experienced players. The game is also widely known for it's amazing tutorial stage, The Great Plateau. This section of the game perfectly eases the player into the world and teaches the player how the game works in a "show not tell" way. Another innovative aspect of the game is the runes. The player is given five powerful "runes," superpowers if you will. You are able to manipulate any metal object with Magnesis, summon remote detonated bombs, pause any movable object or enemy with Stasis, summon ice blocks on any body of water with Cryonis, and even take pictures with a camera. These runes combined with your bow, sword, shield, and paraglider make for a truly free and interesting gaming experience.

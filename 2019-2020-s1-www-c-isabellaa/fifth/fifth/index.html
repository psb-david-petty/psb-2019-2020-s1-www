Trees!

Trees are vital to our planet. They provide almost a third of all oxygen (the great majority comes from marine plants, so take care of the ocean, too). One tree produces 260 pounds of oxygen every year, on average. They can also be huge carbon sinks. A carbon sink is something that emits CO2 slower than it removes it from the atmosphere. Among carbon sinks, oceans and forests are great examples.

Not only do forests have a big role in cleaning up the atmosphere, but they're also the homes of 80% of known land animal species. If forests are lost, these animals' homes are lost.

Trees are in danger

Deforestation, forest fires, and droughts all put forests at risk. Let's protect the environment, animals, and their habitats.
